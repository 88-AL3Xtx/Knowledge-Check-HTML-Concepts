Pressure Washing

is the use of high-pressure water spray to remove loose paint, mold, grime, dust, mud, and dirt from surfaces and objects such as buildings, vehicles and concrete surfaces. The volume of a mechanical pressure washer is expressed in gallons or liters per minute, [1] often designed into the pump and not variable. The pressure, expressed in pounds per square inch, pascals, or bar, is designed into the pump but can be varied by adjusting the unloader valve or using specialized nozzle tips. Machines that produce pressures from 750 to 30,000 psi (5 to 200 MPa) or more are available.

Windows that needed cleaning became higher as buildings became higher. At first, washers cleaned skyscraper windows by standing on the window ledge and holding onto the frame. Later, leather safety belts attached to anchor bolts were introduced and then scaffolds. Restoring Floors

Over time, hardwood floors can become scratched, stained, or faded due to regular wear and tear. Refinishing your floor allows you to restore its original luster. By choosing a new stain color or finish, you can also change the overall appearance of your floor, making it more suitable to your personal taste and design preferences. Trends in flooring also change. Many of our customers have refinished their floors to achieve a lighter, white oak look. If the floor is very dark or is red oak and additional step can be added to the refinishing process, bleaching. This removes the pink and red hues in the minerals of red oak and helps removed the stain trapped in the minerals to produce a beautiful light stained or natural floor.



Floor Polish

It’s a common question among homeowners and cleaning enthusiasts: Is floor polish the same as floor finish? While these terms are often used interchangeably, they refer to different products that serve distinct purposes. Understanding the differences and knowing when to use each can significantly impact the longevity and appearance of your floors.Definitions of Floor Polish and Floor Finish Floor polish is typically a liquid substance applied to a floor to create a shiny, reflective surface. It enhances the aesthetic appeal and can provide a protective layer against minor scratches and scuffs. Floor finish, on the other hand, is a more robust product designed to deliver a durable, high-gloss surface. It’s often used in commercial settings or high-traffic areas because of its strength and resilience.



Garage Painting

Personally, for an old garage that you are not making into a show place, I would just use a dust brush on the lower areas and a soft bristle broom on the higher places and the ceiling, working from the top down. Hosing off drywall would not be recommended since the outer paper layer will absorb water and wick into the inner core. it would take some time to dry out and too much water would damage the gypsum interior. This, of course if the walls/ceiling have never been painted before. If your old garage already has multiple layers of paint, they would be more impervious to water but using a hose, IMHO, would be too much. Using a good primer before your top color coat is a wise move. While painting the ceiling the same color/sheen as the walls is not common because a sheen on the ceiling will exaggerate flaws and be more reflective, it's a garage after all and it depends on what level of quality you are looking for.Cutting in different colors at the ceiling line is really not all that difficult, even for the inexperienced and even without using painters tape. Try it and I bet you will be pleased that it's not as hard or as time consuming as you think. You're going to have to use a brush anyway since the roller won't reach into the tight wall/ceiling angle.

Typical roles and responsibilities within a pressure washing team: Pressure Washer Technician: Operates pressure washing equipment to clean various surfaces, applying detergents and adjusting pressure levels as needed.
